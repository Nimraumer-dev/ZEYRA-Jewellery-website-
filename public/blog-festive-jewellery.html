Jewelry Trends for Eid, Diwali & Festive Occasions

March 05, 2025 By Festival Desk

Festive seasons do not necessarily mean eating, family and fashion - it is a celebration of culture, tradition and expression. The theme in this story is jewelry. Whether it's the glowing lamps of Diwali or the celebratory joy of Eid, your choice of ornaments defines your festive spirit.

This year, festive fashion is seeing a return to "Legacy Glamour." People are gravitating towards pieces that tell a story—designs inspired by Mughal architecture, temple patterns, and vintage royal courts.

  • Statement Jhumkas: No festive look is complete without a pair of bells. Look for oversized Jhumkas with intricate meenakari work or delicate pearl drops.
  • Maang Tikkas: These are making a huge comeback even for non-bridals. A small, elegant Tikka can elevate a simple kurta into a festive ensemble.
  • Stacked Bangles: Instead of one heavy kangan, try stacking different varieties of bangles. Mix metals, colors, and textures for a bohemian yet traditional wrist look.
  • Contrast Gemstones: Wearing jewelry that contrasts with your outfit is the new bold. A deep green emerald set on a pastel pink lehenga creates a stunning visual pop.

Remember, the best festive jewelry is the one that lets you move, dance, and celebrate with ease. Artificial jewellery offers the perfect solution—high-fashion looks with lightweight materials, so you can focus on the festivities rather than the weight of your accessories.
